GA4GH Machine-Readable Consent Guidance — Data Use Ontology (DUO) Reference
[SAMPLE SEED FILE — for prototyping only, not an official GA4GH publication]

Overview

The Data Use Ontology (DUO) is a structured vocabulary that enables machine-readable
encoding of data use conditions, facilitating automated compatibility checking between
dataset use restrictions and researcher data access requests.

DUO terms are hierarchical and can be combined to express complex use restrictions.
Each term is identified by a stable identifier in the format DUO:XXXXXXX.

DUO:0000001 — No Restriction (NRES)

Data is available for use under open access conditions, with no restrictions on use.
This term applies to fully de-identified summary-level datasets published under open
science principles.

DUO:0000004 — General Research Use (GRU)

Data is available for use in any research project. This term does not restrict the
disease area, methodology, or population studied. Users must comply with the general
terms of the applicable Data Use Agreement. Research must remain bona fide and
scientifically valid.

DUO:0000006 — Health/Medical/Biomedical Research Use (HMB)

Data use is limited to health, medical, or biomedical research. Social science,
economic, or non-health research uses are excluded. Studies must have a demonstrable
aim of improving human health outcomes.

DUO:0000007 — Disease Specific Research Use (DS)

Data use is limited to research on a specified disease or group of diseases.
The disease(s) must be specified in the Data Use Agreement or accompanying
metadata annotation. Research that extends beyond the specified disease indication
requires a new or amended access request.

Application guidance:
- The disease scope must be documented at the dataset level.
- Users must not expand the disease scope without DAC approval.
- Combination studies that include the specified disease within a broader cohort
  analysis may be permissible — consult the DAC.

DUO:0000011 — Population Origins or Ancestry Research Prohibited (POA)

Data may not be used for research into population origins or ancestry. This restriction
is applied to protect specific communities from potential stigmatisation.

DUO:0000012 — Genetics Studies Only (GSO)

Data use is limited to genetic studies only, including but not limited to GWAS,
sequencing, and genotyping studies. Non-genetic analyses of associated phenotypic
data may be permitted as ancillary analyses — consult the DAC.

DUO:0000015 — No General Methods Research (NGMR)

Data may not be used as a reference dataset for the development of general analytical
methods, such as bioinformatic tool development or algorithm training, unless this is
the primary stated use of the dataset.

DUO:0000018 — Not For Profit Use Only (NPU)

Data use is limited to not-for-profit organisations. Commercial entities must seek
a separate license. Collaborations between not-for-profit and commercial partners
require DAC approval and execution of a separate commercial use agreement.

DUO:0000019 — Publication Required (PUB)

Users are required to publish results arising from use of this data. Publication
must occur within 24 months of the primary analysis, unless an extension is agreed
with the DAC. Open-access publication is preferred.

DUO:0000020 — Collaboration Required (COL)

Users must collaborate with the data originator(s) or a designated expert in the
relevant disease area. The terms of collaboration, including authorship, must be
agreed before access is granted.

DUO:0000021 — Ethics Approval Required (IRB)

Formal ethics/IRB approval must be obtained and documented before data access begins.
Users must provide proof of current ethics approval at the time of application and
must notify the DAC of any changes to ethics approval status.

DUO:0000025 — Time Limit on Use (MOR)

Data use is restricted to a defined time period, specified in the Data Use Agreement.
Users must destroy or return data at the end of the approved period, unless renewal
is requested and granted.

DUO:0000026 — User Specific Restriction (US)

Data use is restricted to specific named users. Any change in named users requires
notification to and approval from the DAC.

DUO:0000027 — Project Specific Restriction (PS)

Data use is restricted to the specific project described in the access application.
Use for related but distinct projects requires a new application.

DUO:0000028 — Institution Specific Restriction (IS)

Data use is restricted to the named institution(s) specified in the Data Use Agreement.
Data may not be transferred to third-party institutions without prior DAC approval.

DUO:0000029 — Return to Database or Resource (RTD)

Users are required to submit results, including variants, analysis outputs, or derived
datasets, back to the original database or resource within a defined timeframe.

DUO:0000042 — General Research Use and Clinical Care (GRU-CC)

Data is available for use in general research and for routine clinical care.
This term explicitly permits use beyond research contexts, including diagnostic and
therapeutic applications.

Combining DUO Terms

DUO terms are designed to be combined to create fine-grained use conditions.
Example combinations:
  - DUO:0000007 + DUO:0000021 = Disease-specific research requiring ethics approval
  - DUO:0000006 + DUO:0000018 = Health research, not-for-profit only
  - DUO:0000004 + DUO:0000019 = General research use with publication required

Compatibility Checking

Automated compatibility checking should determine whether a requested use matches
all applicable DUO terms. A request is compatible only if it satisfies every
restriction term applied to the dataset.
