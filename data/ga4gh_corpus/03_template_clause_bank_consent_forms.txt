GA4GH Consent Clause Bank — Template Clauses for Genomic Research
[SAMPLE SEED FILE — for prototyping only, not an official GA4GH publication]

Introduction

This clause bank provides model language for use in informed consent forms,
data use agreements, and participant information sheets for genomic research.
Clauses are organised by topic area. Institutions should adapt these clauses
to comply with applicable local law and ethics requirements.

Data Sharing

Template clause (broad consent, open sharing):
"Your de-identified genetic and health information may be stored in a secure
database and shared with qualified researchers worldwide for any future health
and medical research. Your information will not be identified with your name
or other direct identifiers. Researchers who access your data must agree to
keep it secure and use it only for health research."

Template clause (controlled access):
"Your genetic information will only be made available to researchers who apply
for and receive approval from an independent Data Access Committee. Approved
researchers must sign a Data Use Agreement that limits how they may use your
information and requires them to protect its confidentiality."

Template clause (dataset-level DUO annotation):
"This dataset is annotated with the following Data Use Ontology (DUO) terms:
DUO:0000007 (Disease Specific Research Use) and DUO:0000021 (Ethics Approval
Required). Researchers must restrict their use to the specified disease area
and must provide proof of current ethics board approval."

Protection of Privacy

Template clause (de-identification):
"Before sharing your information, we will remove or encode your name, address,
date of birth, and other information that could directly identify you. However,
because your genetic information is unique to you, we cannot guarantee that
your identity could never be inferred by someone who combined your genetic data
with other publicly available information."

Template clause (re-identification risk disclosure):
"Genomic data carries a small but real risk of re-identification. The research
team and all approved data recipients must not attempt to re-identify you. If
a data recipient attempts to re-identify participants, their access will be
revoked and the matter reported to the relevant authorities."

Template clause (privacy safeguards):
"Your data will be stored on encrypted servers with access restricted to
authorised personnel only. Access to your individual-level data is logged and
audited. In the event of a data breach, you will be notified in accordance with
applicable law."

Recontact

Template clause (recontact for incidental findings):
"If researchers discover information that may be important to your health —
such as a genetic variant linked to a serious treatable condition — you may
be contacted by the research team. You may indicate below whether you wish
to be recontacted for this purpose."

Template clause (recontact for additional consent):
"If researchers wish to use your information for a purpose that is not covered
by this consent form, they may ask to contact you again to seek your permission.
You are under no obligation to respond or to agree."

Template clause (no recontact):
"You will not be individually contacted about the results of research using your
information. Aggregate results may be published in scientific journals or
presented at conferences."

Sample Storage

Template clause (long-term sample retention):
"Your biological sample (blood/saliva) will be stored indefinitely at a secure
biobank facility. It may be used for future research related to human health
and disease. You may request destruction of your sample at any time by
contacting the biobank."

Template clause (limited retention):
"Your biological sample will be stored for a period of [X] years following the
completion of this study. After this period, all remaining samples will be
destroyed unless you provide separate consent for extended storage."

Template clause (secondary use of samples):
"Any remaining portion of your sample may be used for future research studies,
subject to ethics approval. If a future study requires access beyond the
scope of this consent, researchers must either seek a waiver from the ethics
board or seek your separate consent."

Return of Results

Template clause (individual research results):
"Research results will not be returned to individual participants as a general
rule, because results from research studies have not been validated for clinical
use. However, if a result of clear and significant health importance is
discovered and could be clinically verified, the research team may contact you
through your healthcare provider."

Template clause (aggregate results):
"Aggregate findings from this research will be made publicly available through
scientific publications, conference presentations, and the study website.
You will not receive personalised results from your individual data."

Withdrawal

Template clause (right to withdraw):
"You may withdraw your consent at any time without giving a reason and without
any negative consequences for your care. To withdraw, please contact [study
contact]. After withdrawal, no new access to your individual data will be
permitted. However, data already incorporated into completed analyses cannot
be recalled."

Governance

Template clause (oversight):
"Use of your data is overseen by an independent Data Access Committee (DAC)
that reviews all applications and ensures that data is used only for approved
purposes. The DAC composition and review criteria are publicly available at
[URL]."

Template clause (DUO compliance):
"This study uses the GA4GH Data Use Ontology (DUO) to describe the conditions
under which your data may be used. Your data is tagged with the DUO term(s)
noted in the dataset documentation. Approved researchers must comply with all
applicable DUO restrictions."
