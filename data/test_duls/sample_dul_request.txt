Project Title: Genomic Determinants of Cardiovascular Disease in Diverse Populations

Institution: University of Genomic Sciences, Department of Cardiology

1. Project Overview

We request access to the controlled-access whole-genome sequencing dataset (cohort ID:
COHORT-0042) to investigate the genetic architecture of cardiovascular disease (CVD)
in populations of African, South Asian, and East Asian ancestry.

The study aims to identify novel genetic variants associated with coronary artery
disease, atrial fibrillation, and hypertension using genome-wide association study
(GWAS) methodology. We expect to enrol approximately 50,000 participants across
three contributing sites.

2. Data Use and Purpose

2.1 The requested data will be used exclusively for the study of cardiovascular
disease as defined in Section 1 above. We will not use the data for other disease
areas without seeking a separate amended access request from the Data Access Committee.

2.2 All analyses will be conducted within the secure computational environment
provided by our institutional HPC cluster. Data will not be transferred to personal
devices or external storage media.

2.3 We will not attempt to re-identify any participant.

3. Ethics and Regulatory Approval

3.1 This study has received ethics approval from the Institutional Review Board of
the University of Genomic Sciences (Protocol No. IRB-2025-CVD-007, approved
15 August 2025). A copy of the approval letter is attached.

3.2 Approval is current and valid through 31 August 2026, subject to annual renewal.

4. Data Security

4.1 All data will be stored on encrypted servers within our institution's secure
research computing environment. Access is restricted to named project members listed
in Appendix A.

4.2 We will maintain access logs and conduct quarterly security reviews.

4.3 In the event of a data breach, we will notify the Data Access Committee and
the institution's data protection officer within 72 hours.

5. Consent and Participant Protection

5.1 We confirm that the original participants in the source cohort consented to
broad use of their genomic data for health and medical research.

5.2 We will comply with all conditions of the Data Use Agreement.

6. Cross-Border Data Transfer

We note that one of our three contributing sites is located in Germany. We will
comply with applicable EU GDPR requirements for international data transfers. Data
processed in Germany will remain within EU jurisdiction and will not be transferred
outside the EU without separate legal basis.

7. Publication and Dissemination

7.1 We will publish findings in open-access peer-reviewed journals.

7.2 All publications will acknowledge the contributing cohorts and the Data Access
Committee in accordance with the acknowledgment guidelines.

7.3 We will submit summary statistics and any novel variants identified to a public
repository within 12 months of primary publication.

8. Declaration

We confirm that the information provided in this data use letter is accurate and
complete. We agree to comply with the applicable Data Use Agreement, the GA4GH
Framework for Responsible Data Sharing, and all applicable laws and regulations.
